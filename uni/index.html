Uni Device

A digital device that supports adolescents with MDD (Major Depressive Disorder), combined with the concept of emotional design, aims to encourage users to engage in cognitive activities and social interactions. It was developed during my undergraduate program and resulted in the production of a research article.

Introduction

Among depression cases in society, those occurring during adolescence are more frequent due to the biological, psychological, and social changes adolescents go through. This project focuses on a study that proposes the development of a digital device to mitigate the effects of mild MDD (Major Depressive Disorder) in adolescents, seeking social reintegration and prevention of worsening symptoms through the use of Human-Computer Interaction principles and Affective Ergonomics.

Goal 1: Mitigate the effects of MDD

Goal 2: Promote social relationships

Goal 3: Prevent worsening

Goal 4: Apply Hedonomics

1. Immersion

During the preliminary Immersion stage, bibliographic and documentary research was conducted in order to build a theoretical foundation based on expert authors in the field. From the in-depth Immersion phase, the key agents involved in the life of an adolescent with mild MDD (Major Depressive Disorder) were identified, and based on that, research procedures were applied.

Using the netnography technique, a structured questionnaire was specifically applied to ten adolescents diagnosed with mild MDD to map post-crisis effects (short, medium, long term, from the perspective of the individual, family, professionals, and friends) and habits related to the use of digital interactive devices.

Example of the applied form

Later, with a focus group of three adolescents of different ages, topics such as interpersonal relationship level (family members, classmates, friends, best friends, romantic partners, and strangers) were discussed, and the themes related to the origin of the condition in each patient and its effects were reinforced. Structured interviews were also conducted with two groups defined as relevant factors in adolescents' lives. A structured interview with three professionals in psychology and psychiatry was conducted to gather data on alternative treatments, recommendations commonly made after diagnosis, and factors that aggravate the disorder.

Finally, a structured interview was conducted with the general public (around 30 people, including family and non-family members of adolescents with depression), in order to collect insights from the public perspective on depressive disorder and their level of knowledge about the topic.

Focus Group

From the focus group, it was possible to understand that the origins of the reported disorders stem from different factors, namely: bullying, family aggression, and romantic relationships. It is clear that all participants showed abrupt behavioral changes after the events, and through diagnosis it was possible to define what should be done as treatment. All were diagnosed with mild MDD, but only one remained in treatment; the others were not interested because they did not want to “worry their families.”

All participants showed low self-esteem and eating disorders, and did not feel satisfied with their own bodies due to the disorder. It was reported that this condition, in addition to being recurrent and inactive, cannot be predicted, as there is no specific trigger for a melancholic state. Therefore, adolescents are prone to feel the effects of depression at any moment. In two of the three cases, there were frequent thoughts related to suicide, resulting from dissatisfaction with their feelings and lack of interest in daily activities; however, no suicide attempts were reported.


2. Creation

Aiming to implement a digital device as the project solution, usability and emotional ergonomics concepts were applied in its design, and a framework of design attributes was defined, since they address self-fulfillment needs. Through brainstorming combined with the synthesis of data generated in the immersion stages and co-creation with adolescents with depressive disorder, it was possible to generate alternatives for the theme. Then, using insight cards, generated ideas were filtered and possible solutions were organized by specific categories, and finally combined with guiding criteria.

With the help of a positioning matrix, it was possible to analyze the best alternatives to develop for each persona and create a prototype. The criteria established for the project are those that provide the focus envisioned at the beginning of its development. Through them, it becomes visible which factors carry greater weight and what needs to be solved. The term persona refers to the use of fictional characters that present the same behavior patterns as the main people involved in the problem. In this project, three different personas were used: one representing an adolescent with MDD, another representing a family member (the father), and the other a person from the social circle (a friend).

Identity

3. Prototyping

Socialize

To encourage user engagement with the device, it is possible to use points earned through usage to exchange for skins that can be used by the character.

Skins designed for the character

Encouragement of Activity Practice

Using the “Fitness” menu, the device will present features to the user such as “Walk,” “Meditate,” “Jumping Jacks,” etc. There will be random moments when the user will receive suggestions at alternating times, with phrases from the character such as: “How about taking care of your skin today?”. These suggestions can be accepted; however, because they are not monitored by the character, they will not result in large amounts of friendship points.

Example of the "Walk" feature

Interact with the user

The device character will respond to user voice commands through expressions similar to emojis, since these are commonly used among the target audience. It will also allow users to accumulate points and use them to unlock new appearances for the character.
